# Statistical Analysis Report of Oxidative Stress Markers

## Workbook Overview

The consolidated Excel file contains a single sheet named Consolidated_Data. This sheet contains the Clove and Bay Leaf datasets stacked vertically. The Clove group contains CL1 to CL15. The Bay Leaf group contains BL1 to BL15. Control groups and reference drug groups are included.

## Stacking and Grouping Details

Data from both experimental runs were stacked. Clove run samples occupy rows 3 to 31. Bay Leaf run samples occupy rows 32 to 57. The sample IDs in the first column were fully written out to prevent confusion. For example, raw indices in the Bay Leaf run were expanded to BL 1 through BL 15, IBU 1 through IBU 4, MG 1 through MG 3, and NG 1 through NG 3. Clove samples CL 16 and CL 17 from the second run were pooled with CL 1 through CL 15, yielding a single Clove group of 17 animals.

## Statistical Observations

Model induction during the Clove run resulted in statistical differences in total protein, SOD, CAT, and MDA levels. The activity of SOD decreased from 1.6934 U/g Prot in the normal control to 1.3108 U/g Prot in the model control. Catalase activity similarly dropped from 1.2774 U/g Prot to 0.9805 U/g Prot. The MDA level dropped from 0.2991 mol/g Prot in the normal control to 0.1370 mol/g Prot in the model control, showing an unexpected downward trend.

Administration of the pooled clove extract did not restore antioxidant enzyme levels. The SOD activity in the treated group was 1.1535 U/g Prot, which was lower than in the model control. GPx activity in the treated group was 1.5567 U/g Prot, also lower than the model control level of 2.1189 U/g Prot. This GPx decrease was statistically significant. Furthermore, the MDA level in the clove treated group was 0.4470 mol/g Prot, indicating elevated lipid peroxidation. Ibuprofen treatment significantly reduced the MDA level to 0.0578 mol/g Prot.

No statistical significance was achieved for any of the comparisons in the Bay Leaf run. The differences between the normal group and the model group remained non-significant across all parameters. For instance, the mean SOD activity of the normal group was 1.3069 U/g Prot compared to 1.4010 U/g Prot in the model group.

The administration of Bay Leaf extract and Ibuprofen failed to produce statistically significant changes compared to the model group. The standard deviations and standard errors in these groups were large relative to the differences between group means, which explains the high p-values.

## Statistical Methodology

All values were analyzed using Student's independent two-sample t-test to evaluate statistical differences between groups. Standard error of the mean (SEM) was calculated by dividing the sample standard deviation by the square root of the group sample size. Statistical significance was defined as a p-value less than 0.05.
