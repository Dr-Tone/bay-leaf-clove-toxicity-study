## PHASE 5: DISCUSSION — CHAPTER FOUR

Interpret your results in the context of existing literature.

### Structure:
1. **Restate key findings** (1 paragraph) — what did this study show?
2. **Acute toxicity discussion**: Compare LD50 classification with existing literature on clove/eugenol toxicity. Discuss safety margin. Relate to OECD guideline.
3. **Antidysmenorrhea discussion**: Explain how SAE inhibited writhing. Compare with ibuprofen. Discuss mechanisms (prostaglandin inhibition? Ca²⁺ channel blockade? TRPV1 modulation?).
4. **Mechanistic insights**: Connect to known eugenol pharmacology (COX-2, NF-κB, calcium channels). Cite the papers already in the reference list.
5. **Comparison with previous studies**: Reference similar findings from the literature review section.
6. **Study limitations**: What could have been done better? (sample size, no phytochemical fingerprinting, no pure eugenol comparison, etc.)
7. **Implications**: What do these findings mean for future research or clinical use?

### Key rules:
- Do NOT introduce new results here
- Do NOT cite papers that aren't in the reference list
- Every claim about eugenol mechanism must trace back to a cited paper
- Refer to `no_bullshit.md` rules

## SUGGESTED WORK ORDER (PRIORITY)

Do NOT work in document order. Work in this order instead:

1. **Chapter 1 (Introduction/Literature Review)** — this is the biggest section and lays the foundation
2. **Chapter 2 (Materials and Methods)** — needs to be written before/alongside lab work
3. **Chapter 3 (Results)** — only after experiments are done
4. **Chapter 4 (Discussion)** — only after results
5. **Chapter 5 (Conclusion)** — only after discussion
6. **Abstract** — write last (summarizes everything)
7. **Front matter** — write last (title page, acknowledgment, dedica
8. **Appendices** — assemble as you go
